Weddings in 2021

Come Jan 2020, while we walked into the final year of the decade, little did we know our lives as we know them were going to go under such a major change. The onset of Covid-19 sent us all in a tizzy, shutting us down, locking us in our homes for months at stretch. While we went through all the stages disbelief, unacceptance, deliberation, adaptation and even the Instagram challenges, the world was changing, Zoom meeting rooms turned into the new conference halls, whipping up the Dalgona coffee became the new coffee date and social media and OTT platforms became our best friends.

While the world was busy debating over Seema aunty and her antics in the Netflix series “Indian Matchmaking” there were many a hundreds of Indian couples and families deliberating, discussing and innovating ways to get married. The mind boggling questions that lay in front of these families was, how would they manage the quintessential Indian wedding, fondly called ‘The Big Fat Indian Wedding’ without making it BIG? How would they reduce the easy 300 member guest list to a mere 50-100 one? How would the arrangements take place with a nation-wide lockdown in place? How would the guests attend, would it be safe?

As the saying goes, necessity is the mother of all invention. The wedding industry evolved too, and really quickly at that. Guests were now attending weddings virtually on all sorts of platforms, events were planned and managed to suit the needs of the times with the outlook of keeping the guests entertained while retaining the true essence of a marriage – the celebration of love, togetherness and culture. If you are planning a wedding, or are a wedding photographer or makeup artist who often find themselves pondering over the question, whether small and intimate weddings the way forward, has the face of Indian weddings changed for once and for all, you aren’t alone.

When asked whether this was the end of the over the top wedding ceremonies by Indianexpress, famous fashion designer Falguni Shane Peacock said, “the girl wants to look like a princess on her wedding day, and that will not change”, various pandits and pujaris are now adapting the online weddings because that’s what the couples and their families want. What is quickly becoming a trend, was the unthinkable just a couple of years ago, couples are starting their wedded bliss by getting married on zoom and other platforms, yes Zoom Weddings are a thing now. While this is all extraordinary and unusual, the flavour of the Indian Wedding still remains the same, the couple surrounded by family and their loved ones amidst lots of good food, great decoŕ and music.

We, the Aisshwarya Banquet Halls family have always had our customers needs and experience at the heart of our service, in these unprecedented times of Covid we stand with our patrons and pray for everyone’s health and safety. We continue to cater to our customers for events and ceremonies while abiding to all the current local as well as national rules and norms and welcome you with open arms and warm smiles.

Whether small and intimate weddings are here to stay is a question that remains to be seen in the future, but what will not change is how Indian weddings are an amalgamation of rituals, traditions, dance, music and a plethora of emotions, of not just two individuals but two families coming together to celebrate the couple’s first day of forever. So as you log into that zoom invite or walk into that wedding venue the next time, remember it is a celebration of your loved ones, and as long as you are a part of it, that’s all that matters right? Let the wedding festivities begin!!
